# Task 5: Cybercrime Awareness

## Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is a common type of cybercrime in which attackers try to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, bank details, or OTPs by pretending to be a trusted organization. This type of attack usually happens through emails, SMS messages, or fake websites that look very similar to real ones.

For example, a student may receive an SMS claiming to be from a bank, stating that their account will be blocked unless they update their details immediately. The message contains a link that looks genuine. When the student clicks on the link, they are taken to a fake website designed to look exactly like the official bank website. The student then enters their login credentials and OTP, thinking it is safe. However, this information is directly sent to the attacker, who then uses it to access the bank account and transfer money.

Phishing attacks mainly target students, working professionals, and people who are not aware of online threats. The consequences of such attacks can be serious, including financial loss, identity theft, and misuse of personal information.

This case study shows that users must always verify links and avoid sharing sensitive information online without proper verification.
